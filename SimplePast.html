Simple past

Información General.

The past simple, or simple past, is one of the most important verb tenses in the English language and, unlike in Spanish, where past tense verbs have very different conjugations, in English there are verbs that retain their form in both the present and past, and the more irregular ones have as few as three forms.

So, in theory, for a native speaker of Spanish, regular and irregular verbs in English should represent a less complex task. In this article you will find some keys that will help you in their understanding, especially in their formulas or structures and in their pronunciation.

Definición

When to use itThe past simple is used for sentences in which the idea, act or state has already been completed.

It could have finished five minutes ago or five centuries ago, the time of completion does not matter, the only important thing is that we are talking about something that no longer happens or exists in the present.

To emphasize that it is a fait accompli, it can be accompanied by context, for example:

  • A year ago, I ate in that restaurant.
  • In 1970, The Beatles split up.
  • When you were a child you liked apples.

Note: These context sentences can go before or after the main sentence. For example:

  • I ate in that restaurant a year ago.

There are verbs whose past tense does not always express a past situation. The best example of this is could, the past tense of can, which is used to formalize questions, for example:

  • Could you close the door, please? (¿Podrías cerrar la puerta, por favor?)
